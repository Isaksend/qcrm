The adoption of a software as a service model requires careful consideration of the cloud infrastructure and the regulatory environment.
\section{Data Residency and Law No. 94-V}
Law No. 94-V on personal data and their protection mandates that the storage and processing of personal data of Kazakhstani citizens must occur on servers physically located within the territory of Kazakhstan \cite{kazakhlaw2013personal}. This regulatory requirement restricts the use of global cloud solutions that do not have local data centers, supporting the project decision to utilize a controlled deployment within the country \cite{mordor2026ict}.
\section{Analysis of Local Cloud Providers}
The Kazakhstan ICT market has seen a 69 percent revenue increase in cloud migration in recent years \cite{mordor2026ict}. Major local providers include Kazakhtelecom, which maintains a 52 percent share of the data center market and offers virtual computing resources \cite{telecom2023report}. Other specialists include QazCloud, which focuses on disaster recovery, and 24saas.kz, which provides virtual data centers specifically tailored for SaaS applications in Kazakhstan \cite{twentyfoursaas2025about}. These local options allow SaaS products to comply with national privacy laws while maintaining low latency for regional users \cite{gcore2025locations}.
