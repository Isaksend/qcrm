\chapter{Introduction}
\label{chap:intro}
%
Customer relationship management is defined in academic literature as a strategic approach that integrates technology, processes, and people to manage and optimize interactions with both current and potential customers \cite{esmat2025study}. In the current digital landscape, these systems have evolved toward predictive models where machine learning and data processing are integrated into the core business architecture to improve commercial results \cite{saha2025crm}. Within this global context, the Republic of Kazakhstan has established a significant digital infrastructure, characterized by an internet penetration rate of 92.9 percent and high levels of online public service delivery \cite{kepios2025digital}.

Despite these advancements, a significant dichotomy persists within the national economy. Micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs) constitute 99.9 percent of registered businesses, yet their contribution to the gross domestic product remains markedly lower than in advanced economies \cite{kazstat2025indicators}. Research indicates that these entities frequently rely on manual tools such as spreadsheets, citing the complexity and high cost of existing enterprise solutions as primary barriers to adoption \cite{astanahub2024implementation}.

The TinyCRM project identifies a specific market gap. There is a lack of lightweight, localized tools for micro-teams of one to five members who are currently underserved by global providers. This segment views manual data entry as their most significant productivity bottleneck, with 86.7 percent of surveyed entrepreneurs highlighting it as a critical pain point. TinyCRM aims to fill this gap by providing a simplified interface for data management while leveraging machine learning for complex analysis. The system utilizes a monolithic architecture built with Python, using the FastAPI framework for request handling and SQLAlchemy for data management.
